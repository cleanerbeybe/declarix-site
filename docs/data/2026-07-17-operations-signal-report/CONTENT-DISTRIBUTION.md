# Link acquisition and distribution plan

## Linkable hooks

1. **The priority-versus-structural gap:** only 50 of 402 qualified accounts were priority-grade current signals, while 352 were strong or structural fit.
2. **The incumbent-system wedge:** 397 of 402 qualified accounts rated 4 or 5 for product fit under a preserve-the-CMS rubric.
3. **The uncertainty ledger:** 489 unresolved rows remained visible rather than being silently excluded or promoted.
4. **The website-language split:** 469 of 854 candidates exposed customs plus workflow terms; keyword evidence routed human review rather than generating automatic leads.

## Reusable assets

- report canonical URL;
- aggregate CSV with definitions and snapshot date;
- 1200 × 630 vector press chart;
- source-register links and visible methodology;
- direct links to the desk calculator, pack checker, and response kit.

## First distribution wave

- publish a short founder analysis around “Hiring is the sharp edge, not the whole customs-operations market” and link to the report;
- offer the chart and CSV to freight/customs trade editors as evidence, not a product announcement;
- cite one finding in relevant registration-consultation commentary and link back to the full method;
- create three LinkedIn cuts, one per finding, using the report chart and a method note;
- invite BIFA/FIATA/HMRC directory corrections through the editorial-policy route without asking for prospect data;
- use the report as the citation target from future customs-operations guides, comparisons, and tool explainers.

## Measurement

- `operations_report_downloaded` by aggregate CSV vs press SVG;
- `operations_report_related_clicked` by calculator, pack checker, registration kit, and workflow route;
- `operations_report_booking_clicked` by masthead vs bottom CTA;
- `operations_report_methodology_viewed` from the hero action;
- `operations_report_shared` by native share vs clipboard.

Do not add campaign-specific query data, contact information, or document values to these events.
