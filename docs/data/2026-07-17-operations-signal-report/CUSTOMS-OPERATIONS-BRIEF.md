# Customs Operations Brief — future publication specification

## Status

**Documentation only. No public signup form is authorized in this phase.**

A newsletter should be published only after a real email provider, consent copy, privacy treatment, unsubscribe handling, event contract, and delivery owner exist. Until then, the report and its downloads remain ungated.

## Reader promise

A concise evidence brief for UK customs-broker and freight-forwarder operations teams: one verified operational signal, one practical desk action, and one source register per issue.

## Proposed issue shape

1. **Signal:** one aggregate, policy, system, or workflow change.
2. **Desk consequence:** the operational question it creates.
3. **Workpaper:** one free tool, checklist, or template.
4. **Source register:** primary links, dates, and what is interpretation.
5. **Numbers call:** optional route for readers with a live workflow to model.

## First four issues

- Hiring shows urgency; workflow evidence shows the larger market.
- Preserve the customs system and remove the document-assembly drag.
- What unresolved public evidence should change in a qualification process.
- How to model declaration labour without inventing an automation rate.

## Preconditions for implementation

- named provider and list owner;
- explicit consent wording and privacy-notice update;
- verified double-opt-in/unsubscribe behavior where configured;
- `brief_signup_started`, `brief_signup_completed`, and `brief_unsubscribed` event contract without email properties;
- welcome issue and first two scheduled issues ready before the form is published;
- browser QA of success, error, keyboard, mobile, and unsubscribe states.
