# Source register

Sources were checked on 17 July 2026. Official publisher pages control current requirements, consultation wording, deadlines, and submission instructions.

## Registration response kit

1. HM Revenue & Customs, “Customs intermediaries: introduction of Mandatory Registration”
   <https://www.gov.uk/government/consultations/introduction-of-mandatory-registration-for-customs-intermediaries/customs-intermediaries-introduction-of-mandatory-registration>

   Supports: publication date; 13-week duration; 21 September 2026 deadline; proposed scope, exclusions, minimum requirements, enforcement approach, next steps, 13 consultation questions, partial-response invitation, and official response process.

2. HM Revenue & Customs, “Standard for Customs Intermediaries”
   <https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/standard-for-customs-intermediaries>

   Supports: the existence and status of the separately published industry Standard. The kit keeps the Standard distinct from the proposed mandatory registration framework.

## Customs document pack checker

1. HM Revenue & Customs, “Making a full import declaration”
   <https://www.gov.uk/guidance/making-a-full-import-declaration>

2. HM Revenue & Customs, “CDS Declaration Completion Instructions for Imports”
   <https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/cds-uk-trade-tariff-volume-3-import-declaration-completion-guide/uk-trade-tariff-cds-volume-3-import-declaration-completion-guide>

3. HM Revenue & Customs, “Standard for Customs Intermediaries”
   <https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/standard-for-customs-intermediaries>

These sources support broad preparation themes. The checker does not claim that its 24 prompts are the complete data requirements for every movement or declaration category.

## Freshness rule

- Registration page expires as an “open consultation” resource after 21 September 2026 unless reviewed.
- Update or redirect the page when HMRC closes responses, publishes a response summary, or announces legislation or implementation.
- Recheck the pack-checker source register when import declaration completion guidance or the intermediary Standard changes materially.
