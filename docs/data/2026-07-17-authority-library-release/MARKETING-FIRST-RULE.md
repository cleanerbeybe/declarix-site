# Marketing-first copy and design rule

Date adopted: 2026-07-17

## Non-regression rule

Acquisition pages are marketing and conversion surfaces, not legal contracts. Copy priority is:

1. match the visitor's intent;
2. deliver the useful answer or task quickly;
3. show the practical outcome and next step;
4. support important facts with evidence;
5. place limitations only where they change the visitor's decision or safe use.

Legal, governance, policy, and risk language must never replace the value proposition, lead the hero,
occupy the conversion slot, or turn a clear CTA into defensive prose. Necessary boundaries stay short,
specific, and adjacent to the affected claim or action. They do not become the page's voice.

## QA rejection conditions

Reject a release when it:

- weakens or removes an approved conversion path without evidence;
- moves legal, limitation, or governance language directly under an acquisition hero;
- replaces a concrete operational benefit with institutional or policy prose;
- gates a useful authority asset without an explicit acquisition decision;
- introduces a newsletter or lead form that has no live destination;
- changes the live site's visual identity into generic rounded cards, indigo SaaS styling, or a quiet
  editorial redesign.

The final reviewer checks copy, conversion, SEO, accessibility, responsive layout, and live behaviour as
one release contract. Passing technical SEO alone is not sufficient.
