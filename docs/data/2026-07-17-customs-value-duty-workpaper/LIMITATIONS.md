# Limitations and result boundary

The workpaper performs arithmetic only. It does not:

- decide which customs valuation method or sale applies;
- determine whether an amount must be added, deducted, apportioned, or excluded;
- fetch or select an HMRC or contract exchange rate;
- classify goods or determine a commodity code;
- establish origin, preference, quota, suspension, relief, or evidence eligibility;
- select a duty, excise, levy, or import VAT rate;
- calculate a specific duty amount per weight or unit, or a compound tariff combining that amount
  with an ad valorem percentage;
- determine declaration, payment, postponed-accounting, or VAT-recovery treatment;
- submit, validate, or assess a customs declaration; or
- produce an official HMRC amount.

The browser applies only the ten displayed inputs. The result remains a planning scenario until the
movement facts and current official rules are checked. These boundaries appear beside the result and
source material; they do not displace the tool's value or conversion message.

Amount inputs are limited to 100,000,000 and invoice-currency units per £1 to 0.0001–1,000,000 so
supported scenarios retain penny-level output precision. Movements beyond those planning bounds need
a higher-precision calculation workflow.
