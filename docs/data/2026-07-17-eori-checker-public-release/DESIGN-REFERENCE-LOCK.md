# Design reference lock — public GB EORI checker

## Build target

The current live Declarix report and customs-value workpaper system is the source of truth. This is
an extension of the existing Paper World, not a redesign.

## Preserve

- Archivo for display and body copy; IBM Plex Mono for operational labels, identifiers, source
  stamps and timestamps.
- Deep teal ink/docket surfaces, warm off-white paper, amber breadcrumb band, green reserved for
  the primary action and registry-confirmed state.
- Square, low-radius geometry; one-pixel rules; an asymmetric hero/ledger; dense but accessible
  source and result records.
- A value-first hero, the one-field checker above explanatory content, and the existing 20-minute
  numbers-call path.
- The checker result before every conversion ask. No email gate and no dead newsletter form.

## Borrow only

- The result ledger hierarchy from the customs-value workpaper: a decisive dark answer surface,
  a source/timestamp row, then practical next actions.
- The original research report's compact source-register and related-resource link treatment.

## Role rules

- Green is CTA or confirmed-registry-success only. It does not become a decorative page wash.
- Amber identifies wayfinding or a needs-attention state. It never implies registry validity.
- The deep-teal result surface carries the answer. Accuracy copy sits beside that answer rather
  than becoming a legal wall above the form.
- Business names and addresses, when HMRC returns consented details, remain plain escaped text.

## Media strategy

No bitmap or decorative illustration is needed. The EORI input, normalized identifier, registry
stamp, timestamp and source record are the visual evidence.

## Reject

Generic rounded-card grids, indigo, a soft serif/editorial redesign, decorative gradients,
governance-first hero copy, fake official branding, dead forms, gated results, or a format-only
answer presented as a registry result.

## Token commitments

Reuse the current site's variables and typography. Add only state-specific colors that meet
contrast requirements on their assigned surface. Do not redefine the established Paper World
canvas, type scale, borders, radius or primary button treatment.
