## 1. The one job

Multimodal ended today. For the next 3 weeks, almost every visitor is a **warm broker verifying you** after a stand conversation, a follow-up email, or a forwarded intro. They arrive on a phone, sceptical, giving you 90 seconds.

The site's job: **survive the credibility check and convert it into either (a) one anonymised ugly pack sent to you, or (b) a booked 20-minute numbers call.** Everything else is decoration.

This is why v2 is **one page** (`DCLRX-H1`), not five. One perfectly crafted scroll beats five okay pages, concentrates the animation budget, and means a follow-up link always lands on the full argument. Privacy page aside, nothing else ships in v2.

## 2. The big idea — "The entry assembles itself"

The homepage **is the product demo**. The whole page is styled as a customs form (the locked "we speak fluent paperwork" brand), and as the visitor scrolls, a real-looking messy job — two amalgamated invoices, a packing list, a bill of lading, an email thread, a phone-photo of a delivery note — **assembles itself into a CDS-ready pack** in front of them: fields lift off the documents, tether lines draw to their evidence, missing commodity codes get flagged and researched, the finished pack slides into a slot marked *Sequoia*, and a green stamp thunks down.

Why this beats "Apple gloss":
- **Apple's money isn't in gradients; it's in one obsessive idea executed with typographic and motion precision.** For this audience, glossy AI-startup aesthetics *cost* trust (locked anti-goal). The $10M feel comes from craft applied to *their* visual language — a broker recognises an entry form before reading a word.
- **Every animation is diegetic.** Nothing floats, glows or parallaxes for its own sake. Things *stack, scan, lift, tether, flag, seat, stamp, shred* — verbs from the clearance desk. That restraint is what reads as expensive.
- **The scroll answers the #1 objection ("AI will get me fined") without saying a word**: the visitor literally watches the evidence trail being built and the clerk's check being made easy. Show, then tell.

## 3. Competitive read (July 2026) → the angles

| **CargoWise** | "One platform for everything", enterprise consolidation | Validates the mid-market fear of rip-and-replace. Declarix rides that fear: *your stack, untouched*. Also: whatever they file in, the rekeying from customer paperwork still exists upstream — Declarix sits above all of them. |
| **ASM Sequoia / Descartes e-Customs** | The incumbent filing systems | Not competitors — the destination. Naming them fluently *is* the trust move. |

**Three angles nobody else owns (lead with these):**
1. **Verification speed, not extraction accuracy.** Everyone shouts "99%". Brokers hear "1% of my entries get me penalised." Declarix sells the opposite: *"We don't ask you to trust it. We make it fast to check."* The evidence view is the hero feature because it respects the clerk instead of replacing them — and the clerk keeps their name on the entry.
2. **Nothing stored.** Every competitor holds data (dashboards, audit archives, master data). For firms handling clients' commercial invoices, `RETENTION: 0 DAYS` is a trust weapon *and* it kills the "another system holding our data" objection before it forms.
3. **Product-as-proof front door.** Every competitor's CTA is "watch/book a demo." Declarix's is the event close, productised: **send one anonymised ugly pack, get the entry-ready pack back within one working day.** Zero decks, zero logins, one email — which also demonstrates the actual ingestion mechanic (forward an email) in the act of buying.

## 4. Funnel psychology — the commitment gradient

Warm visitor → **feels the pain replayed** (Box 2 opens on the 08:52 inbox, their morning) → **watches the fix happen** (assembly scene; endowment: by the end they've mentally used it) → **objections die in order** (evidence → any file → your system → nothing stored → numbers) → **two exits, both cheap**:

- **Rung 1 — "Send one ugly pack"** (lowest friction, highest proof; the sceptic's CTA). Risk reversal stated as fact: *if we can't structure it, you've lost nothing.*
- **Rung 2 — "Book the 20-minute numbers call"** (the primary conversion; Cal.com inline). Twenty minutes, not thirty — matches the event playbook's short-ask discipline.

CTAs repeat at: sticky header (always), end of assembly scene, after numbers box, final Box 9, mobile sticky bottom bar after 40% scroll. Multiple placements, **one ladder** — the two rungs never compete because they're sequential in the buyer's head (prove it → price it).

Honest scarcity: pilot capacity line ("onboarding a limited number of pilot brokers this quarter") — true for a two-founder company, no fake countdowns.

**Multimodal window:** a date-gated bar (auto-hides after 31 July) — "Met us at Multimodal? Mention it when you book; we'll bring your stand notes to the call." Attribution: `?src=` URL param flows into the Cal booking metadata and mailto subject, extending the DCLRX form-ref lead-source system.

## 6. What changed vs DCLRX-WEB-1 (deliberate evolutions)

1. **Motion budget expanded** from "one stamp moment per page" to a full scroll narrative — but the stamp remains the *single animated signature* (climax of the assembly scene); the pilot guarantee now uses a static printed stamp. All new motion obeys the diegetic rule above. Reduced-motion users get the complete argument with zero pins.
2. **Call length 30 → 20 minutes**, aligning site with the event playbook's ask discipline.
3. **Second CTA rung added** (ugly pack) — WEB-1's "one CTA" principle survives as "one ladder"; mailto was already the sanctioned secondary channel, now it's productised.
4. **Five pages → one page** for v2 scope (follow-up-link economics + craft density). The W1/S1/P1/C1 refs become section anchors (`#evidence`, `#security`, `#pilot`, `#book`) and can be re-expanded later.
5. **New locked claims** from product reality: any file · email-forward ingestion · nothing stored · evidence per field · flags + research · amalgamations handled · output for Sequoia/Descartes.
6. **Manifest-orange single use** reassigned to the Multimodal bar until 31 July, then reverts to the pilot-slots counter.

Everything else — tokens, Archivo/IBM Plex Mono, banned words, "AI" ≤ once per page (spent in Box 5), numbered boxes, form refs, no gradients, no shadows, sentence-case buttons — carries over untouched.

## 7. Measurement (PostHog, cookieless)

- Warm-visit → conversion (ugly-pack mailto click OR booking) — target ≥ 5% combined in the Multimodal window.
- 5-second test: hero states what + for whom + price shape without scrolling.
- Scroll-depth to Box 9 on mobile > 55%; assembly-scene completion > 70%.
- Field-tap interactions on the live pack (proxy for endowment).
- `src` attribution on every booking/mailto (multimodal / linkedin / qr / referral).
